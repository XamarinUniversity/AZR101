AZR101 Introduction to Azure

Exercise 2: Explore the Azure pricing calculator

In this exercise, you will use the Azure Pricing Calculator to estimate the costs for using Azure products.

Open the online pricing calculator

  1. Open a web browser and browse to the Azure Pricing Calculator. You can also browse to the Azure site and click the Pricing link at the top of the page.

Select services to estimate

  1. Start by adding an App Service; this is in the Featured section of the calculator.
  2. We want to store and retrieve some data; add a SQL Database from the Databases section.
  3. We also want to send push notifications to devices, so switch to the Web and Mobile section and select Notification Hubs. Alternatively, you can type Notification Hubs in the search box.

Customize the estimates

We are ready to view our estimates. The estimates will be below the products box.

  1. Scroll down to find the Your Estimate boxes.

In this section, you'll see that each service is shown in a separate form in which you can customize the region, tier, instance and hours. At the bottom of each box, e.g. App Service, you'll see the cost for that service with the settings you chose.

  1. Customize the settings in your App Service to see how it effects the cost.

View the results

  1. Scroll to the bottom of the service estimates and find the total estimate for your services.
  2. Try changing the numbers in the form to see the effect on the total price.
  3. Add a new server component of your choice by selecting one in the products form at the top of the page and see how it effects your total.

Exercise summary

In this exercise, you used the Azure pricing calculator to estimate the monthly cost of using of Azure components.
